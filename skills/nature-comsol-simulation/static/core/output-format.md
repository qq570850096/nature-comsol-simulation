# Output Format

Use a compact publication-facing format. Adapt section names to the user's task,
but keep missing inputs and reviewer risk visible.

## Default Structure

1. **Detected axes** - One short line.
2. **Claim-evidence map** - What is supported, suggested, and unsupported.
3. **Ready-to-use output** - Results, Methods, response text, checklist, or
   review plan as requested.
4. **Missing inputs** - Specific fields needed to strengthen the output.
5. **Reviewer-risk flags** - Severity, reason, and fix.
6. **Optional next step** - Only when it materially improves the paper.

## Language Rules

- Use "the simulation predicted", "the model indicated", or "under the stated
  assumptions" for model-derived results.
- Use "consistent with" for supported interpretation and "suggests" for bounded
  inference.
- Avoid "proves", "demonstrates mechanism", "confirms real-world performance",
  and "validates" unless validation is supplied.
- Do not bury uncertainty in footnotes. Put missing inputs and risk flags where
  the author can act on them.

## Reviewer-Risk Scale

| Severity | Meaning |
|---|---|
| High | Likely to trigger major revision or rejection if central to the claim |
| Medium | Likely reviewer question; fixable with reporting or targeted analysis |
| Low | Presentation or traceability issue that should be cleaned before submission |
