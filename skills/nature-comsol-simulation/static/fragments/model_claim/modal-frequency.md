# Model Claim: Modal Frequency

Use when claims depend on eigenfrequency, natural frequency, mode shape,
resonance avoidance, harmonic response, damping, or vibration performance.

Check:

- Boundary conditions and mass assumptions can dominate modal frequency.
- Report mode number, frequency, mode shape, normalization, and whether rigid
  body modes were removed.
- For resonance claims, compare excitation frequency range with modal response.
- For damping or amplitude claims, eigenfrequency alone is insufficient.
- Validate with experimental modal analysis or benchmark data when possible.

Wording: "the model predicted an upward shift in the first bending mode" is
safer than "the design avoids resonance" without operational excitation data.
