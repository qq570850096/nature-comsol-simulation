# Model Claim: Optimization and Design

Use when claims depend on optimized geometry, topology, parameter tuning,
comparative design, trade-off analysis, or design recommendation.

Check:

- Define objective function, constraints, design variables, bounds, and solver.
- Distinguish optimized numerical objective from practical design superiority.
- Require sensitivity or robustness checks for manufacturing tolerance, material
  variation, and boundary-condition uncertainty.
- Compare against a fair baseline under identical conditions.
- Validate key optimized outputs experimentally or with an independent model when
  the design claim is central.

Wording: "the optimized configuration reduced the simulated peak stress under
the specified load case" is safer than "the optimized design is superior" unless
validated.
