# Simulation Domain: Thermal-Mechanical Coupling

Use for thermal stress, expansion, heat-induced deformation, thermoelasticity,
thermal cycling, and coupled heat-transfer/solid-mechanics papers.

Check:

- Coupling direction: one-way thermal load, fully coupled thermoelasticity, or
  temperature-dependent material properties.
- Heat source, convection, radiation, contact conductance, initial temperature,
  and thermal boundary assumptions.
- Material properties: thermal expansion, conductivity, heat capacity, elastic
  modulus, and temperature dependence.
- Mesh/time-step convergence for thermal gradients and stress concentrations.
- Whether thermal stress is compared with strength, fatigue, or failure criteria.

Allowed strong claim: predicted stress/deformation under declared thermal loads.

Risky claim: thermal safety, damage prevention, or operating lifetime without
representative thermal cycling and validation.
