# Simulation Domain: Biomechanics

Use for implant, tissue, bone, vessel, cartilage, device-body interaction,
orthopedic, dental, cardiovascular, or other biological mechanical simulations.

Check:

- Patient/sample geometry source, segmentation, smoothing, and anatomical
  assumptions.
- Tissue material model and parameter provenance; biological variability matters.
- Loading and constraints: gait, bite force, blood pressure, compression,
  physiological motion, or simplified laboratory loading.
- Validation against experiments, cadaver tests, imaging, clinical measurement,
  or literature values when biological performance is claimed.
- Ethics/data provenance when patient-specific geometry or imaging is used.

Allowed strong claim: simulated mechanical distribution under a defined anatomy
and loading scenario.

Risky claim: clinical benefit, safety, or treatment superiority without
patient-level validation and uncertainty analysis.
