# Simulation Domain: Fluid-Structure Interaction

Use for FSI, pressure-loaded deformation, flow-induced stress, valve/vessel
mechanics, aeroelastic or hydroelastic coupling, and multiphysics interface
claims.

Check:

- Coupling type: one-way pressure mapping, two-way FSI, partitioned coupling, or
  monolithic COMSOL multiphysics.
- Fluid regime, boundary conditions, turbulence/laminar assumptions, and mesh
  motion or remeshing strategy.
- Interface mapping, coupling convergence, time-step sensitivity, and structural
  mesh quality under deformation.
- Whether flow outputs and structural outputs are validated separately or jointly.
- Whether pressure, shear, deformation, and stress are interpreted within the
  same operating condition.

Allowed strong claim: coupled response under declared fluid and structural
assumptions.

Risky claim: physiological, aeroelastic, or device-performance conclusion
without realistic flow boundary conditions and validation.
